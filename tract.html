Jesus - The Missing Piece

Many people think that the universe just happened. That the big bang just occurred for no reason. That humans were created simply by random processes of evolution.

This is not true. God created the world.[1] He did this through his son, Jesus Christ[2], also called “the word” of God[3]. “He is before all things, and in him all things hold together.”[4] He is “the alpha and the omega”.[5] In other words, he is the origin, the sustainer, and also the final purpose of all the pieces of the universe. Because of Jesus, the universe is not random or chaotic, but obeys mathematical laws. It is finely tuned. Humans came into existence on a planet of life - because God planned it. The bible - a book written by humans, but inspired by God, says that God is a “God who hides himself”[6]. But nevertheless he was and is always there working often invisibly behind the scenes through his son Jesus Christ.[7]

Because of their disobedience, Adam and Eve were cast out of paradise[8] and punished with death[9]. All their descendants inherited a predisposition to sin from them[10], and became destined for eternal death[11][12].

God wanted to rescue man from these consequences[13]. He promised he would one day send a special person, a mysterious “messiah” to rescue mankind[14]. He said it was important that people trusted in and obeyed the messiah when he came[15]. Through his prophets, he told people in advance what the signs would be so that everyone could recognise him[16], and even at what time he would come[17]. But many things were still hidden, a “mystery”[18]. The prophets and even angels in heaven thought hard about this, trying to decode God’s clues[19].

One clue was that the messiah would be born in Bethlehem in Israel[20]. And then when the messiah was about to be born, 2000 years ago, God sent a special star to lead the way to him.[21] Wise men from the East followed the star and found the young Messiah, Jesus Christ, in Bethlehem.[22] Yes, the Eternal Word had become a human being of flesh and blood.[23] The invisible “missing piece” had suddenly become visible at a particular time in history.[24][25]

When the Messiah grew up, he started doing miracles, which were further signs to prove to people who he was.[26] Some people recognised that he was the one we must follow[27], but others - in particular the religious leaders of the time - did not recognise that he was the Messiah[28][29] or want to follow him[30], but rather they wanted to kill him. They didn’t recognise him[31] because the Devil had blinded their eyes to the truth[32].

Eventually they did kill him.[33] They arranged for the Romans, who ruled the world at that time, to nail him to a cross of wood, murdering him in the most painful manner.[34]

But this did not catch God out unawares. God had already planned that this would happen[35] so that he could forgive humans[36] and enable them to come back to a friendship with Him again[37].

Jesus was then resurrected from the dead[38], which was a sign that humans would eventually be resurrected also[39]. He instructed his followers (“disciples”) to tell others about him and persuade them to follow him too[40]…

One of the religious leaders, called Saul, hated the Christians and went about imprisoning and killing them.[41] One day a blinding vision of Jesus knocked him down. The vision asked Saul, “Why are you persecuting me?” But Saul didn’t recognise who it was until the vision revealed that he was Jesus.[42] The final piece of the puzzle fell into place for Saul, and he became a follower of Jesus too.[43]

One of the places where Saul tried to make disciples was Athens in Greece.[44] They had lots of temples there and lots of different idols - gods of stone and metal and wood that they bowed down to.[45] But they did not know about Jesus - the only one truly worthy of worship. However, there was one altar that Paul noticed, which had written on it “To an unknown God”.[46] Maybe the Athenians had a feeling that there was a mysterious God behind the scenes that they didn’t yet know about but that nevertheless required their worship?

Saul said “The one you worship as unknown, I now declare to you.” And he told them all about Jesus.

Saul explains: “Although God overlooked the ignorance of earlier times, He now commands all people everywhere to repent.”[47] In other words, men must abandon the bad things they are doing, and other things they are worshiping, and trust in Jesus to rescue them[48], obeying him as their Lord[49]. This is so important as otherwise they are destined for judgment and eternal torment in hell[50][51]. For those who believe, there is eternal life awaiting and joy with Jesus in heaven.[52][53]

So what about you? Look around at the idols where you are - temples and statues but also shopping centres and sports arenas? Family and romantic relationships? God demands that all things must take second place to Jesus. Will you turn away from worshiping other things and make Jesus number one in your life?

If you want to do this, then pray the following prayer with your whole heart and Jesus will accept you.

“Dear Jesus, I am sorry for the bad things I have done in my life and the other things I have put before you. Please forgive me. I turn away from these things and put my trust in you, making you Lord of my life. Thank you for your forgiveness and acceptance, which you promise to all who come humbly to you.”
